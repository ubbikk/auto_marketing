# Raymond Carver
# Minimalist. What's NOT said carries weight. Short declarative sentences.
# Everyday language doing heavy lifting.

## SAMPLES

This blind man, an old friend of my wife's, he was on his way to spend
the night. His wife had died. So he was visiting the dead wife's relatives
in Connecticut. He called my wife from his in-laws'. Arrangements were made.

---

I was in bed when I heard the gate. I listened carefully. I didn't hear
anything else. But I heard that. I tried to wake Cliff. He was passed out.
So I got up and went to the window.

---

My eyes were still closed. I was in my house. I knew that. But I didn't
feel like I was inside anything.

---

It was like nothing else in my life up to now.

---

'Go ahead, bub, draw,' he said. 'Draw. You'll see. I'll follow along with you.
It'll be okay. Just begin now like I'm telling you.'

---

The two kids were very much in love. On top of this they had great ambitions.
They were always talking about the things they were going to do and the places
they were going to go.

## KEY TECHNIQUES

- Short, clipped sentences
- Spare dialogue
- Ordinary details that suggest larger meaning
- Endings that don't resolve â€” they just stop
- Trust the reader to fill gaps

## ANTI-PATTERNS (what Carver never does)

- Over-explanation
- Neat resolutions
- Flowery language
- Stating emotions directly
- Too much interiority
