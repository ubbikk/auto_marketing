WHY THESE RULES EXIST:
The banned words aren't arbitrary. Research shows "delve," "leverage," "tapestry,"
and similar words increased 700-1500% in usage after ChatGPT launched. Readers
unconsciously pattern-match on them — even one triggers the "this is AI" response.
The structural patterns (emoji openers, numbered lists, em-dash overuse, snappy
triads like "clear, concise, and compelling") are the formatting fingerprint of AI.
Your content will be automatically rejected if it contains violations, so use
original language. If a word feels like it belongs in a corporate memo, cut it.

NUMBER PATTERNS — THE CRITICAL TELL:
AI defaults to specific-but-round numbers: "47 projects," "200 clients," "73.2%."
Real humans say "like 50," "maybe two-thirds," "a few hundred," "I don't know,
over a thousand." Use human number patterns — approximations, hedged figures,
ugly real numbers. If you catch yourself writing a number that ends in 0 or 7,
rewrite it.
